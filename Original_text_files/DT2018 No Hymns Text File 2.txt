Monday, January 1 — Psalm 1
Genesis 1:1–2:3; Matthew 1:1–17

You must therefore be careful to do as the Lord your God has commanded  you; you shall not turn to the right or to the left. Deuteronomy  5:32

Jesus said, “My Father is glorified by this, that you bear much fruit and become my disciples.” John 15:8

Gracious God, as we walk the road of this new year, we give thanks that you are the one who illuminates the way. May our way be your way and may each of our steps glorify you. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Tuesday, January 2 — Psalm 2
Genesis 2:4–25; Matthew 1:18–25

The Lord has sent me to provide for those who mourn in Zion—to give them a garland instead of ashes, the oil of gladness instead of mourning, the mantle of praise instead of a faint spirit. Isaiah 61:3

Jesus said, “Come to me, all you that are weary and are carrying  heavy burdens, and I will give you rest.” Matthew 11:28

Healer, Redeemer, Binder of wounds, through your love all things are made new. We give thanks that in you we are made whole. In the name of the one who calls us beloved. Amen.


Wednesday, January 3 — Psalm 3
Genesis 3,4; Matthew 2:1–12

Return, O my soul, to your rest, for the Lord has dealt bountifully with you. Psalm 116:7

My God will fully satisfy every need of yours according  to his riches in glory in Christ Jesus. Philippians  4:19

Sacred Friend, your presence is always with us. When we are lonely, you offer companionship. When our bodies and souls are weary, you offer refreshment. May we follow your example as we provide presence for one another. Amen.


Thursday, January 4 — Psalm 4
Genesis 5; Matthew 2:13–23

It was not because you were more numerous than any other people that the Lord set his heart on you and chose you—for you were the fewest of all peoples. It was because the Lord loved you. Deuteronomy  7:7-8

God chose what is low and despised in the world, things that are not, to reduce to nothing things that are, so that no one might boast in the presence of God. 1 Corinthians 1:28–29

God, forgive us when we focus on the things that divide rather than the things that unite. May we, as Jesus’ disciples, sow seeds of love in the world and be bound together by the joy of serving you. Amen.


Friday, January 5 — Psalm 5
Genesis 6:1–7:10; Matthew 3

On the day I called, you answered me, you increased my strength of soul.  Psalm 138:3

And after you have suffered for a little while, the God of all grace, who has called you to his eternal glory in Christ, will himself restore, support, strengthen,  and establish you. 1 Peter 5:10

Gentle Guide, when we stray from the path that you so graciously set before us, let us always listen for your voice as it calls us back to life in you. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Epiphany of the Lord

Watchword  for the Epiphany — Arise, shine; for your light has come, and the glory of the Lord has risen upon you. Isaiah 60:1

Epiphany of the Lord — Isaiah 60:1–6; Psalm 72:1–7;10–14
Ephesians 3:1–12; Matthew 2:1–12

Saturday, January 6 — Psalm 6
Genesis 7:11–8:22; Matthew 4:1–11

See, the Lord God comes with might, and his arm rules for him. Isaiah 40:10

By the tender mercy of our God, the dawn from on high will break upon us, to give light to those who sit in darkness and in the shadow of death. Luke 1:78–79

Spirit of the living God, you call us to be your witnesses to a hurting world. Strengthen us to be salt and light as we share your love with neighbors near and far. In the name of Jesus we pray. Amen.


First Sunday after the Epiphany

Watchword  for the week — Ascribe to the Lord the glory of his name; worship the Lord in holy splendor. Psalm 29:2

Sunday, January 7 — Genesis 1:1–15; Psalm 29
Acts 19:1–7; Mark 1:4–11

The Lord said to Moses, “I will raise up for them a prophet like you from among their own people; I will put my words in the mouth of the prophet.” Deuteronomy  18:18

Philip found Nathanael and said to him, “We have found him about whom Moses in the law and also the prophets wrote, Jesus son of Joseph from Nazareth.” John 1:45

Word of life, you continue to speak peace into the world. Give us ears to hear and hearts to understand the often-unlikely sources from which your truth is proclaimed. Amen.


Monday, January 8 — Psalm 7:1–9
Genesis 9; Matthew 4:12–25

When their breath departs, they return to the earth; on that very day their plans perish. Psalm 146:4

Can any of you by worrying add a single hour to your span of life? Matthew 6:27

Dearest Jesus, you have assured us that you have overcome the things of this world. When we feel overwhelmed, help us to cast our cares onto you. In grateful praise we pray. Amen.


Tuesday, January 9 — Psalm 7:10–17
Genesis 10:1–11:9; Matthew 5:1–16

From your lofty abode you water the mountains; the earth is satisfied with the fruit of your work. Psalm 104:13

God has not left himself without a witness in doing good—giving you rains from heaven and fruitful  seasons, and filling you with food and your hearts with joy. Acts 14:17

Artful Weaver, you have created this world with more than enough. May we share your abundance through our witness to all whom cross our path. In your Son’s name we pray. Amen.


Wednesday, January 10 — Psalm 8
Genesis 11:10–12:9; Matthew 5:17–26

O Lord, I will praise you; though you were angry with me, your anger is turned away, and you comfort me. Isaiah 12:1 (NKJV)

God has destined us not for wrath but for obtaining salvation through our Lord Jesus Christ. 1 Thessalonians  5:9

Lord, we all long for communion with you. Draw us close when we are unable to feel you near. Comfort us in our doubts. Open our eyes to recognize your spirit in one another. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Thursday, January 11 — Psalm 9:1–10
Genesis 12:10–13:18; Matthew 5:27–42

Surely he has borne our infirmities and carried our diseases; yet we accounted him stricken, struck down by God, and afflicted. Isaiah 53:4

For our sake God made him to be sin who knew no sin, so that in him we might become the righteousness of God. 2 Corinthians 5:21

Lamb of God, forgive us when we are unwilling to sacrifice our individual comforts for the greater good. Help us to build one another up in your love.
In the name of Jesus we pray. Amen.


Friday, January 12 — Psalm 9:11–20
Genesis 14,15; Matthew 5:43–6:4

The Lord God will take away the disgrace of his people from all the earth. Isaiah 25:8

Rejoice in hope, be patient in suffering, persevere in prayer. Romans 12:12

Gracious God, you call us as your family into fellowship with you. Thank you for our sisters and brothers in Christ, who join us along the way. In never-ending praise. Amen.


Saturday, January 13 — Psalm 10:1–11
Genesis 16,17; Matthew 6:5–18

For as the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my ways higher than your ways and my thoughts than your thoughts. Isaiah 55:9

Now I know only in part; then I will know fully, even as I have been fully known. 1 Corinthians 13:12

Holy Wisdom, place in us the desire to align our will with yours. As we seek the guidance of your spirit, transform our thoughts and actions to radiate your love. In Christ’s name we pray. Amen.


Second Sunday after the Epiphany

Watchword  for the Week — How weighty to me are your thoughts, O God! How vast is the sum of them! Psalm 139:17

Sunday, January 14 — 1 Samuel 3:1–10,(11–20); Psalm 139:1–6,13–18
1 Corinthians 6:12–20; John 1:43–51

The Lord was with Joseph; and whatever he did, the Lord made it prosper. Genesis 39:23

Not that we are competent of ourselves to claim anything as coming from us; our competence is from God. 2 Corinthians 3:5

On this Sabbath day, Lord, may our worship bring you glory. May our fellowship bring us joy. May our rest reflect your peace. In your holy name we pray. Amen.


Monday, January 15 — Psalm 10:12–18
Genesis 18; Matthew 6:19–34

Only fear the Lord, and serve him faithfully with all your heart; for consider what great things he has done for you. 1 Samuel 12:24

His mercy is for those who fear him from generation to generation. Luke 1:50

God, it is a comfort to know that we can bring our deepest joys and needs to you in prayer. Guide us in your example of unceasing prayer so that our
whole being brings glory to you. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Tuesday, January 16 — Psalm 11
Genesis 19:1–29; Matthew 7:1–12

You shall not set your desire on your neighbor’s house or anything that belongs to your neighbor. Deuteronomy  5:21 (NIV)

Jesus said, “Take care! Be on your guard against all kinds of greed; for one’s life does not consist in the abundance of possessions.” Luke 12:15

Forgive us, Lord Jesus, when we fail to remember that the craving of material goods leads to spiritual poverty. May our energies be directed into investing in the treasures of your spirit. Amen.


Wednesday, January 17 — Psalm 12
Genesis 19:30–20:18; Matthew 7:13–23

They will be mine. I will spare them as a man spares his own son who serves him. Malachi 3:17 (NASB)

To all who received him, who believed in his name, he gave power to become children of God. John 1:12

God of abundance, you have equipped us to love and serve all of your children. Through the power of your Holy Spirit, lead us to find and welcome your children who may feel excluded from your blessings. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Thursday, January 18 — Psalm 13
Genesis 21; Matthew 7:24–8:4

You are the trust of all the ends of the earth and of the farthest sea. Psalm 65:5 (NASB)

Jesus Christ came and proclaimed  peace to you who were far off and peace to those who were near. Ephesians 2:17

Precious Jesus, you call us to be peacemakers in our homes, in our communities, and in our world. Help us to speak kindness into discord, comfort into mourning, and hope into hopelessness. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Friday, January 19 — Psalm 14
Genesis 22; Matthew 8:5–22

The promises of the Lord are promises that are pure, silver refined in a furnace on the ground, purified seven times. Psalm 12:6

You will shine among them like stars in the sky as you hold firmly to the word of life. Philippians  2:15–16 (NIV)

Lord, you never promised us that life would be easy. However, you did promise to be with us through it all. May our trust in your promise witness to the joyful hope that we have in you. In the name of Jesus we pray. Amen.


Saturday, January 20 — Psalm 15
Genesis 23:1–24:25; Matthew 8:23–34

God said, ‘Let there be light’; and there was light. Genesis 1:3

God, who said, “Let light shine out of darkness,”  made his light shine in our hearts. 2 Corinthians 4:6 (NIV)

Illuminate our minds, Lord Jesus, to recognize the needs of those around us. Open our hearts to share the burdens of our sisters and brothers as we hold one another in the light. In Christ’s name we pray. Amen.


Third Sunday after the Epiphany

Watchword  for the Week — Trust in him at all times, pour out your heart before him; God is a refuge for us. Psalm 62:8

Sunday, January 21 — Jonah 3:1–5,10; Psalm 62:5–12
1 Corinthians 7:29–31; Mark 1:14–20

I give thanks to your name for your steadfast love and your faithfulness; for you have exalted your name and your word above everything. Psalm 138:2

Jesus prays: “I made your name known to them, and I will make it known, so that the love with which you have loved me may be in them, and I in them.” John 17:26

Blessed Redeemer, on this holy day may our worship of you spill out of the sanctuary and into every aspect of our lives. May all that we do give testimony to your unifying love. In Christ’s name we pray. Amen.


Monday, January 22 — Psalm 16:1–6
Genesis 24:26–66; Matthew 9:1–13

He knows the secrets of the heart. Psalm 44:21

These are the words of the first and the last, who was dead and came to life: I know your affliction and your poverty. Revelation  2:8–9

God, you know us so intimately that even the number of hairs on our heads are counted. Thank you for the assurance that we need not hide any part of our lives from you. You love us always. In your holy name we pray. Amen.


Tuesday, January 23 — Psalm 16:7–11
Genesis 25; Matthew 9:14–26

The Lord was with Samuel and let none of his words fall to the ground. 1 Samuel 3:19

Paul wrote: The word of the Lord has sounded forth from you not only in Macedonia and Achaia, but in every place your faith in God has become known, so that we have no need to speak about it. 1 Thessalonians  1:8

We are blessed by the people whom you have brought into our lives to help us to grow in faith. Bless us now, Lord, to share the faith that we have been taught with those who are waiting to learn. In Christ’s name we pray. Amen.


Wednesday, January 24 — Psalm 17:1–7
Genesis 26; Matthew 9:27–38

Help, O Lord, for there is no longer anyone who is godly; the faithful have disappeared from humankind. Psalm 12:1

Pray for us that God will open to us a door for the word, that we may declare the mystery of Christ. Colossians 4:3

We can speak of your love, O God, but if we do not act in love, our words are all for naught. Strengthen us so that our actions witness to the words of praise that we speak. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Thursday, January 25 — Psalm 17:8–15
Genesis 27:1–29; Matthew 10:1–16

Do not withhold good from those to whom it is due, when it is in your power to do it. Proverbs 3:27

How does God’s love abide in anyone who has the world’s goods and sees a brother or sister in need and yet refuses help? 1 John 3:17

Gracious Provider, help us to deeply see the needs of others. Gift us with your vision—a vision that looks beyond circumstances and sees into the soul of a fellow child of God. Stir our hearts and hands to help. In Christ’s name we pray. Amen.


Friday, January 26 — Psalm 18:1–6
Genesis 27:30–28:9; Matthew 10:17–25

You are my servant, I have chosen you and not cast you off. Isaiah 41:9

The gifts and the calling of God are irrevocable. Romans 11:29

Bless our congregation, Lord, as we honor the unique gifts that everyone brings to worship and service. May our community of faith rest in the assurance that wherever you call us to go, your Holy Spirit goes before us. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Saturday, January 27 — Psalm 18:7–15
Genesis 28:10–29:14; Matthew 10:26–42

Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life, and I shall dwell in the house of the Lord my whole life long. Psalm 23:6

Jesus said, “Very truly, I tell you, anyone who hears my word and believes him who sent me has eternal life, and does not come under judgment, but has passed from death to life.” John 5:24

What a joy it is, blessed Savior, to abide in you! You shepherd us with love that knows no bounds and mercy that embraces our missteps. May all of our days be lived in deep communion with you. In your holy name we pray. Amen.


Fourth Sunday after the Epiphany

Watchword for the Week — The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom; all those who practice it have a good understanding. Psalm 111:10

Sunday, January 28 — Deuteronomy 18:15–20; Psalm 111
1 Corinthians 8:1–13; Mark 1:21–28

Wait for the Lord; be strong, and let your heart take courage; wait for the Lord! Psalm 27:14


You have heard of the endurance  of Job, and you have seen the purpose of the Lord, how the Lord is compassionate and merciful. James 5:11

Merciful God, in times of turmoil and distress we pray for the patience and courage that we sometimes need just to make it through the day. Provoke us to ask for help from you and from those around us. Amen.


Monday, January 29 — Psalm 18:16–24
Genesis 29:15–30:24; Matthew 11:1–10

I will give them one heart, and put a new spirit within them. Ezekiel 11:19

If anyone is in Christ, there is a new creation: everything old has passed away; see, everything has become new! 2 Corinthians 5:17

Giver of life, through the death and resurrection of your Son, you have breathed redemption into the dark corners of our lives. May we, your church, carry your healing breath out into world to speak life to the hopeless. Amen.


Tuesday, January 30 — Psalm 18:25–29
Genesis 30:25–31:21; Matthew 11:11–24

May God continue to bless us; let all the ends of the earth revere him. Psalm 67:7

I pray that you may have the power to comprehend,  with all the saints, what is the breadth and length and height and depth, and to know the love of Christ that surpasses knowledge,  so that you may be filled with all the fullness of God. Ephesians 3:18–19

Father, may we rest in the assurance of your love as shown to us in Jesus. May we strive to emulate the selfless love of Christ Jesus. Fill us with your spirit and enflame our hearts with your love. Amen.


Wednesday, January 31 — Psalm 18:30–36
Genesis 31:22–55; Matthew 11:25–12:8

I will sing to the Lord, for he is highly exalted. Exodus 15:1 (NIV)

See what love the Father has given us, that we should be called children of God; and that is what we are. 1 John 3:1

All praise to you, holy Parent! You lead and protect us as a loving father. You nurture and guide us as a devoted mother. You cherish us as your precious children. We are blessed to be your family. Amen.


Thursday, February 1 — Psalm 18:37–45
Genesis 32:1–21; Matthew 12:9–21

They did not thirst when he led them through the deserts; he made water flow for them from the rock. Isaiah 48:21

Let everyone who is thirsty come. Let anyone who wishes take the water of life as a gift. Revelation  22:17


Our God, you quench our eternal thirst. You love us as your most precious creation and water is our most precious resource. May we be good stewards of both the water of the well and of your living water. Amen.


Friday, February 2 — Psalm 18:46–50
Genesis 32:22–33:20; Matthew 12:22–32

Will any teach God knowledge, seeing that he judges those that are on high? Job 21:22

All of you must clothe yourselves with humility in your dealings with one another, for “God opposes the proud, but gives grace to the humble.” 1 Peter 5:5

Lord, we ask that you grant us the strength to employ what we know when we share your love. Grant us the grace to admit what we do not know and to have a humble heart for reconciliation. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Saturday, February 3 — Psalm 19:1–6
Genesis 34; Matthew 12:33–45

How precious is your steadfast love, O God! All people may take refuge in the shadow of your wings. Psalm 36:7

Do not abandon that confidence  of yours; it brings a great reward. Hebrews 10:35

Heavenly Father, help us to contemplate our words wisely before speaking. Remind us that our actions will always speak louder. For one day we must account for how we have treated the ‘least of these’ in our very own neighborhoods. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Fifth Sunday after the Epiphany

Watchword  for the Week — Great is our Lord, and abundant in power; his understanding is beyond measure. Psalm 147:5

Sunday, February 4 — Isaiah 40:21–31; Psalm 147:1–11,20c
1 Corinthians 9:16–23; Mark 1:29–39

Comfort, O comfort my people, says your God. Isaiah 40:1

The Son of Man came to seek out and to save the lost. Luke 19:10

O Lord, we are constantly in awe of your majesty and power! What great comfort it is to know that it is you that guides and protects us. Build us up to make us more like your Son. Amen.


Monday, February 5 — Psalm 19:7–14
Genesis 35:1–36:8; Matthew 12:46–13:9

You shall meditate on the book of the law day and night, so that you may be careful to act in accordance with all that is written in it. Joshua 1:8

Blessed are those who hear the word of God and obey it! Luke 11:28

Dear Lord, in the silence, let us hear you. May we take time to pause today—to give thanks and meditate on your will for the church. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Tuesday, February 6 — Psalm 20
Genesis 36:9–43; Matthew 13:10–23

Turn my eyes away from worthless  things. Psalm 119:37 (NIV)

Do not be carried away by all kinds of strange teachings; for it is well for the heart to be strengthened by grace. Hebrews 13:9

Lord, keep us from being of the world as we learn to navigate its dangers and temptations. By your grace, we know our struggle is already won when we have faith in you. To you, O Lord, we pray. Amen.


Wednesday, February 7 — Psalm 21
Genesis 37; Matthew 13:24–35

Then Jacob woke from his sleep and said, “How awesome is this place! This is none other than the house of God, and this is the gate of heaven.” Genesis 28:17

The Word became flesh and lived among us, and we have seen his glory, the glory as of a father’s only son, full of grace and truth. John 1:14

The glory of God is all around us: the wind as it blows through the trees, the song of the bird, the morning dew, a child’s eyes! What wonder it is to be alive. We thank you, Lord. Amen.


Thursday, February 8 — Psalm 22:1–8
Genesis 38; Matthew 13:36–46

They shall live in safety, and no one shall make them afraid. Ezekiel 34:28

A Sabbath rest still remains for the people of God. Hebrews 4:9

You, O Lord, are a refuge. In you, peace and contentment we seek and shall find. In your name we pray. Amen.


Friday, February 9 — Psalm 22:9–21
Genesis 39; Matthew 13:47–58

Deliverance belongs to the Lord; may your blessing be on your people! Psalm 3:8

God has saved us and called us with a holy calling, not according to our works, but according to His own purpose and grace which was granted us in Christ Jesus. 2 Timothy 1:9 (NASB)

God, what great plans you have for us! We may not yet know our part, but we trust your will. Teach us the part we are to play in your kingdom of peace and we will gladly follow! Amen.


Saturday, February 10 — Psalm 22:22–28
Genesis 40:1–41:16; Matthew 14:1–14

The Lord grants peace within your borders. Psalm 147:14

Let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts, to which indeed you were called in the one body. Colossians 3:15

We can no more choose the time in which we live than a dove can choose the way the wind blows as it flies. But we can choose to do our best to reflect you, Lord, in everything we do. Amen.


Last Sunday after the Epiphany
Transfiguration of our Lord

Watchword  for the Week — For we do not proclaim ourselves; we proclaim Jesus Christ as Lord and ourselves as servants for Jesus’ sake. 2 Corinthians 4:5

Sunday, February 11 — 2 Kings 2:1–12; Psalm 50:1–6
2 Corinthians 4:3–6; Mark 9:2–9

Woe to those who go to great depths to hide their plans from the Lord, who do their work in darkness and think, “Who sees us? Who will know?” Isaiah 29:15 (NIV)

Jesus said, “Everyone  who belongs to the truth listens to my voice.” John 18:37

What could be more truthful and honest than pure worship of you, O God? Your glory is bright and we praise your name! Amen.


Monday, February 12 — Psalm 22:29–31
Genesis 41:17–57; Matthew 14:15–24

You make the winds your messengers, fire and flame your ministers. Psalm 104:4

Suddenly from heaven there came a sound like the rush of a violent wind, and it filled the entire house where they were sitting. Divided tongues, as of fire, appeared among them, and a tongue rested on each of them. All of them were filled with the Holy Spirit. Acts 2:2–4

We rejoice for times of abundance, but may we not forget to prepare for times of shortage and strain. Great Provider, with your guidance, may we make the most of both. Amen.


Tuesday, February 13 — Psalm 23
Genesis 42; Matthew 14:25–15:9

The fear of the Lord is the beginning  of knowledge. Proverbs 1:7

Paul wrote: We have not ceased praying for you and asking that you may be filled with the knowledge  of God’s will in all spiritual wisdom and understanding. Colossians 1:9

Let our homes be houses of prayer. Let us remember that prayer is not always what you can do for us, but listening for what you desire us to do as a light unto this world. God we pray. Amen.



Wednesday, February 14 — Psalm 24
Genesis 43; Matthew 15:10–20

Ash Wednesday — Joel 2:1–2,12–17; Psalm 51:1–17
2 Corinthians 5:20b–6:10; Matthew 6:1–6,16–21

Obey me, and I will be your God and you will be my people. Jeremiah 7:23 (NIV)

Jesus said, “If you continue in my word, you are truly my disciples; and you will know the truth, and the truth will make you free.” John 8:31–32

Gracious God, we surrender our pride to you in favor of your grace and truth. May we be humble in giving and not boast about the good we do. In love we pray. Amen.


Thursday, February 15 — Psalm 25:1–7
Genesis 44; Matthew 15:21–28

I know, my God, that you search the heart, and take pleasure in uprightness.  1 Chronicles  29:17

Speaking the truth in love, we must grow up in every way into him who is the head, into Christ. Ephesians 4:15

Lord, you say that if we have faith, even the mountains can be moved. Things may not always turn out ideally, but with faith, we can be secure knowing we are where we belong. Amen.


Friday, February 16 — Psalm 25:8–22
Genesis 45; Matthew 15:29–16:4

God says, “Call on me in the day of trouble; I will deliver you, and you shall glorify me.” Psalm 50:15

Let us therefore  approach the throne of grace with boldness, so that we may receive mercy and find grace to help in time of need. Hebrews 4:16

Lord, forgive us when we fall short. We remember that you alone create, bless and redeem. We cannot ever earn your grace or love, but may we follow your will the best we can. In the name of Jesus. Amen.


Saturday, February 17 — Psalm 26
Genesis 46:1–27; Matthew 16:5–20

My heart is steadfast, O God, my heart is steadfast; I will sing and make melody. Awake, my soul! Psalm 108:1

Are any among you suffering?  They should pray. Are any cheerful?  They should sing songs of praise. James 5:13

Heavenly Father, even in the darkest of times, we still have plently to be thankful for. Above all, we have hope in salvation through the Messiah. In Christ’s name we pray. Amen.


First Sunday in Lent

Watchword  for the Week — Good and upright is the Lord; therefore he instructs sinners in the way. Psalm 25:8

Sunday, February 18 — Genesis 9:8–17; Psalm 25:1–10
1 Peter 3:18–22; Mark 1:9–15

Do not fear, Zion; do not let your hands hang limp. The Lord, your God, is with you, the Mighty Warrior who saves. Zephaniah 3:16–17 (NIV)

Lift your drooping hands and strengthen your weak knees, and make straight paths for your feet. Hebrews 12:12–13

Shown by the rainbow after the flood, we know that you are our God of promise. In you we trust as you guide the way. In your name we pray. Amen.


Monday, February 19 — Psalm 27:1–6
Genesis 46:28–47:31; Matthew 16:21–28

Now if you are unwilling  to serve the Lord, choose this day whom you will serve. But as for me and my household, we will serve the Lord. Joshua 24:15

Think of us in this way, as servants of Christ and stewards of God’s mysteries. 1 Corinthians 4:1

Holy Redeemer, you call us to put our hands to work in the stewardship of the world. Let us always be in awe of its splendor and the beauty of nature around us. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Tuesday, February 20 — Psalm 27:7–14
Genesis 48; Matthew 17:1–13

He has made His wonders to be remembered; the Lord is gracious and compassionate. Psalm 111:4 (NASB)

The cup of blessing that we bless, is it not a sharing in the blood of Christ? The bread that we break, is it not a sharing in the body of Christ? 1 Corinthians 10:16

Hear our prayers, O Lord, when we call aloud to you. Bless us and sustain us in home and school as we live this week. May we reflect you in all that we do. In Jesus’ name we pray. Amen.


Wednesday, February 21 — Psalm 28
Genesis 49; Matthew 17:14–27

The Lord will go before you, and the God of Israel will be your rear guard. Isaiah 52:12

Therefore we must pay greater attention to what we have heard, so that we do not drift away from it. Hebrews 2:1

Great Protector, with you by our side we shall have nothing to fear. Because of this, we can more freely focus on keeping your words close to our hearts. Weigh upon our minds what you desire us to hear. Amen.


Thursday, February 22 — Psalm 29
Genesis 50; Matthew 18:1–14

Does God speak and then not act? Does he promise and not fulfill? Numbers 23:19 (NIV)

Jesus said to Peter: “You of little faith, why did you doubt?” Matthew 14:31

Lord, we see your power in the thunderstorm, but we also see your delicate touch in acts of love. There is so much of you around us—reminding us why we
desire to always be held by you. Amen.


Friday, February 23 — Psalm 30:1–5
Exodus 1:1–2:10; Matthew 18:15–35

This poor soul cried, and was heard by the Lord, and was saved from every trouble. Psalm 34:6
